## Presentation Timing Guide

### Standard Executive Summary (15 slides)
- **Duration**: 15-20 minutes

  - Slides 1-4: Problem & Solution (4 min)
  - Slides 5-8: How It Works (4 min)
  - Slides 9-12: Proof & Implementation (4 min)
  - Slides 13-15: Risks & Next Steps (4 min)

- Focus: Problem, solution, ROI, next steps

- Include all 15 core slides

## Design Recommendations

### Visual Style
- **Clean & Professional**: Minimal text, maximum visuals
- **Large Numbers**: Make metrics stand out (80%, $100K, 5 seconds)
- **Icons Over Text**: Use icons to convey meaning quickly
- **Consistent Colors**: 
  - Primary: Blue (trust, technology)
  - Accent: Green (success, savings)
  - Highlight: Orange (attention, urgency)

### Slide Layout
- **6x6 Rule**: Max 6 bullets, max 6 words per bullet
- **White Space**: Don't crowd slides
- **One Idea Per Slide**: Keep it simple and focused
- **Visual Hierarchy**: Title â†’ Key Point â†’ Supporting Details

### Font Guidelines
- **Titles**: 48-54 pt (Bold)
- **Key Metrics**: 36-42 pt (Bold, colored)
- **Body Text**: 18-24 pt
- **Captions**: 14-16 pt

## Delivery Tips for Executive Audience

### Do's âœ…
- Start with the problem and cost (they care about ROI)
- Use concrete numbers ($80K savings, 80% reduction)
- Keep technical details minimal
- Focus on business value and outcomes
- Show ROI calculation clearly
- Be concise - respect their time

### Don'ts âŒ
- Don't dive deep into technical architecture
- Don't use jargon without explanation
- Don't skip the ROI calculation
- Don't over-promise or exaggerate
- Don't talk about "how" - focus on "what" and "why"

### Engagement Strategy
1. **Hook**: Start with cost/inefficiency (Slide 2)
2. **Show Value**: Quantify the benefit (Slide 4)
3. **Prove It**: Show metrics and capabilities (Slides 7-9)
4. **Make It Real**: ROI and timeline (Slides 12, 14)
5. **Call to Action**: Clear next steps (Slide 15)
